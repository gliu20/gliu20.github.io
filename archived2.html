Philosophy in action

Always making the most of every oppurtunity.

 

I started swimming when I was around 11. I never really thought much of it. My parents had wanted me to swim for the sole purpose of exercising and possibly helping me grow taller. But I never expected that it would also help me grow as a person.

I never expected that it would also help me grow as a person.

At the beginning, I was extremely slow—even slower than little kids. However, I always believed in making the most out of everything—no matter how small it was. I pushed myself because I knew it would be better to make the most with what I had, than to regret later with what I could have done. Nowadays, I swim for Empire swimming, one of the best in Westchester county. It shows why one should never judge anything until one has fully experienced it. Even though I used to dread swimming, it no longer bothers me anymore. In fact, I have now come to love swimming. And it shows how making the most of even the most dreadful things always pays off.

Making the most of even the most dreadful things always pays off.

If we always push ourselves to do our best in every subject, even the lamest subjects might interest ourselves in the end. What I have come to learn is that every diamond was once hidden in a dull rock.

Every diamond was once hidden in a dull rock.

In essence, hard work—no matter where one puts it—always pays off.

My Passion

Programming

 

I remember starting programming when I was only 11. It felt magical to create a simple “hello world” program. From then on, I strove to learn more. In 2017, I began to apply programming to help students and teachers. Realizing that the new schedule confused both students and teachers alike, I created Midway, an extension that reminds staff and students when the period ends. Whenever I create programs, I am overwhelmed with joy, but I am even more happy when it helps others.

Programming make me happy, but I am even more so when it helps others.

In addition, programming is also a mindset. It is teaches one to think critically and logically. Furthermore, programming teaches one to persevere despite struggle. In programming, one will always encounter bugs. But the ability to overcome these bugs is what separates the good from the bad programmer. And these ideas parallel life lessons: that one will always confront obstacles (bugs for programmers), but we will succeed when one always seek to do one’s individual best to overcome these hurdles. Personally, I believe programming is more than a hobby; it is a way of thinking, and above all, it is a passion that allows me to help others.

Programming is more than a hobby; it is a way of thinking, and above all, it is a passion that allows me to help others.
