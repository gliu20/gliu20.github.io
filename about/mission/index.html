My Mission

My Mission is

To help make the world a better place by using programming to help solve real-world challenges.

The Origins of My Mission

I was born in Toronto, Canada, but like many others, my family and I moved to the United States in pursuit of the American Dream.

Originally, we moved to Queens, NY. There, I learned many invaluable life lessons. I learned to be humble and to be grateful. Living among those who were less fortunate revealed to me how much we take most things for granted.

In 2010, my family and I moved to Westchester, NY, and settled down in Scarsdale. Although I had left Queens, my experience there would never leave me. The stark contrast between the wealthy and the less fortunate shocked me—it was something that I could never forget. I had always wanted to help do something about it, but I never had the ability to. It was only until after discovering programming, did I have the ability to help shape the world.

My middle name, Zhen Yu, means shake the world. I had always wondered how I would shake the world, but after discovering programming, I knew the path I would take. For me, coding is like a superpower. It is the means through which anyone can help change the world, and I wanted to live up to my name and do just that.

In Pusuit of the Programming Quest

I remember starting programming when I was only 11. It felt magical to create a simple “hello world” program. From then on, I strove to learn more.

In 2016, I began to apply programming to help senior citizens. I created a web app, Thing Tracker, which helped senior citizens keep track of their stuff.

In 2017, I used programming to help students and teachers. Realizing that the new schedule confused both students and teachers alike, I created Midway, an extension that reminds staff and students when the period ends. Whenever I create programs, I am overwhelmed with joy, but I am even more happy when it helps others.

Not only is programming a tool to help improve people's lives, programming is also a mindset. It is teaches one to think critically and logically. Furthermore, programming teaches one to persevere despite struggle. In programming, one will always encounter bugs. But the ability to overcome these bugs is what separates the good from the bad programmer. And these ideas parallel life lessons: that one will always confront obstacles (bugs for programmers), but we will succeed when one always seek to do one’s individual best to overcome these hurdles. Personally, I believe programming is more than a hobby; it is a way of thinking, and above all, it is a passion that allows me to help others.
