My Values

My Values

Every opportunity is valuable. Making the most of each and every one of them is just my thing.

The Origins of My Values

In Queens, NY, I learned many invaluable life lessons. I learned to be humble and to be grateful. Living among those who were less fortunate revealed to me how much we take most things for granted; the stark contrast between the wealthy and the less fortunate shocked me—it was something that I could never forget.

Living in Queens ingrained within me the mentality that everyone else had—seizing every opportunity. With fewer resources than the more fortunate, we had to be resourceful and make the most of everything.

My Values in Action

When I started swimming around the age of 11, I never really thought much of it. My parents had wanted me to swim for the sole purpose of exercising and possibly helping me grow taller. But I never expected that it would also help me grow as a person.

In the beginning, I was extremely slow—even slower than little kids. However, I always believed in making the most out of everything—no matter how small it was. I pushed myself because I knew it would be better to make the most with what I had than to regret later with what I used to have.

Nowadays, I swim for Empire Swimming, one of the best in Westchester County. It shows why one should never judge anything until one has fully experienced it. Even though I used to dread swimming, I no longer despise it. In fact, I have now come to love swimming. And it shows how making the most of even the most dreadful things always pays off.

If we always push ourselves to do our best in every subject, even the lamest subjects might interest ourselves in the end. I have come to learn that every diamond was once hidden in a dull rock and that hard work—no matter where one puts it—is always rewarded.
